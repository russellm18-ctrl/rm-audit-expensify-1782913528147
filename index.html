You claim "90% reduction in corporate card reconciliation time" and "automated 100% of NetSuite entries" but present zero visual documentation of what that process actually looks like inside the product, leaving the buyer to trust a text claim with no annotated workflow screenshot, no side-by-side before/after interface view, and no visual proof anchoring the Sage Intacct or Workday sync in action. That buyer closes the tab and calls your competitor who shows the dashboard.
  • THE COMPETITIVE EXPOSURE: Ramp and Brex are publishing annotated, role-specific product walkthroughs—actual UI captures with callouts showing approval flows, policy violation flags, and card limit controls—targeting the same finance and accounting segment you're chasing with bullet points. While you list "AI-powered expense categorization" and "Concierge AI" as text features, Ramp is closing mid-market finance teams with visual proof of their automation layer in motion, a gap that translates directly to lost multi-seat contract evaluations where your 15-million-member claim means nothing without visual operational credibility to back it.
  • THE PHOTO-FIRST UPGRADE: Commission a structured UI documentation shoot covering every role-specific workflow—employee receipt capture via the mobile app, manager approval queue, and the two-way QuickBooks/NetSuite sync confirmation screen—captured at screen resolution with strict alt-text protocol: descriptive, keyword-precise, tied to exact feature names and integration partners named on your own site. Each asset gets embedded with consistent file naming (expensify-netsuite-sync-approval-workflow.jpg, not IMG_4872.jpg), descriptive alt attributes referencing the specific integration and user action, and JSON-LD SoftwareApplication markup linking the visual to the feature it documents. This turns your screenshot library from filler into a crawlable, conversion-driving asset that does the selling your current wall of text cannot.
